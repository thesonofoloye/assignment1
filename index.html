About Me

Hi, I'm Al-ameen Oloye, a third year student of Computer Science in the prestigious University of Ilorin . I'm currently a student of Altschool pursuing a diploma in backend engineering (python). My Altschool ID is ALT/SOE/023/2063.

I'm the last born child from a family of six so you'll believe there's no pressure. But of course there is pressure! Seeing siblings do so well in their respectable career fields is enough pressure ever since I've been intellectually aware. That's more than enough reason to avoid being mediocre.

I've had a lot of interests ever since I was young and that is evident im how much I have changed career paths. At some point, I wanted to be a soldier, then a medical doctor, then a banker before I actually settled for a career in computer science. The contrast in these career fields is quite evident and it is testament to the fact that I'm very dynamic. But the one thing that I'm ever grateful for is that I chose a career field in the tech ecosystem. I just discovered it's further proof of me being dynamic as the tech space is ever changing.

There's this flaw about me that I'm aware of : I give up easily. And this has led to a series of inconsistency with several online courses. I drop them off whenever I feel frustrated. Of course, I'm trying to do better and how do I achieve this? I celebrate every small wins as I now believe they are the foundation to that bigger win! I think Tabitha Kavyu mentioned this in one of the onboarding sessions. Glad to see Altschool rubbing off on me.

Why Altschool?

Then there's the question of why Altschool? It was frustrating month of May for me when I applied. It stemmed from a lack of purpose. I was a student of Computer Science and I couldn't even create a simple html form. Of course I have been excelling in the theoretical part of my course but I knew that I needed the hands on skill that sets me apart from the rest. I sure have been taking a lot of online courses but I definitely didn't see them through. Took Google's UX/UI design course in June,2022 and I dropped it frustrated after I couldn't fit in a logo in one of my prototyping lessons. I did UX/UI then because it was no code; I didn't want to code as I felt it was fo the big brains and my insecurity made me believe I'm not a big brain.

So fast forward to January,2023. I was in my second year and we started core programming with Java; it was beautiful. I didn't understand much but then I knew programming was beautiful. Creating lasting technologies for everyday use smacking your fingers on the keyboard definitely was enticing. I believe people like that are God's favourites.

I was doing my research about a field in tech I wanted to pursue when I came across Altschool. And then I knew it was for me. The reviews were captivating and the fact that the course content were sequentially structured was a big deal for me. It was the roadmap I've been searching for.
